Al-Khwarizmi

The Father of Algebra & Architect of Algorithms

An interactive exploration of his life, work, and enduring legacy in Artificial Intelligence.

The Scholar of Baghdad

  • Born around 780 CE in Khwarazm (modern-day Uzbekistan).
  • Flourished as a prominent scholar at the renowned House of Wisdom in Baghdad.
  • This institution was the intellectual heart of the Islamic Golden Age, attracting brilliant minds from across the world.

The Birth of Algebra

  • Authored the groundbreaking book, "Al-Kitab al-mukhtasar fi hisab al-jabr wa'l-muqabala".
  • For the first time, he presented a systematic way to solve linear and quadratic equations.
  • The word "Algebra" is derived from "al-jabr," a key operation described in his book.

Revolutionizing Numbers & Science

  • Hindu-Arabic Numerals: His work was crucial in popularizing the numbers 0-9 in the Middle East and Europe, replacing Roman numerals.

  • Geography: He improved upon Ptolemy's maps and helped calculate the Earth's circumference.

From "Algoritmi" to "Algorithm"

  • The term "algorithm" is derived from the Latinization of his name, Algoritmi.
  • His work established the concept of a clear, finite, step-by-step procedure to solve a problem.
  • This systematic approach is the absolute foundation of all computer programming and artificial intelligence.
