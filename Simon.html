Early Life & A Voracious Intellect

Born: June 15, 1916, in Milwaukee, Wisconsin.

Education: Enrolled at the University of Chicago in 1933, earning his B.A. (1936) and Ph.D. (1943) in political science.

His diverse studies across social sciences and mathematics sparked a lifelong fascination with the core processes of human decision-making.

Redefining Rationality

Bounded Rationality

Simon challenged the classic economic model of a perfectly rational "economic man." He argued that human decisions are limited ("bounded") by:

  • Cognitive (mental) limitations
  • The information available
  • The finite time to decide

Satisficing

This led to the concept of "satisficing." Instead of searching for the optimal solution, people tend to choose the first option that is "good enough" and meets their minimum criteria. This was a more realistic model of human behavior.

A Pioneer's Foray into AI

Logic Theorist (1956)

Considered the first-ever AI program. With Allen Newell and J.C. Shaw, Simon created a program that could prove mathematical theorems. It demonstrated that machines could perform tasks requiring abstract reasoning.

General Problem Solver (1957)

This program introduced means-ends analysis, a core AI problem-solving technique still used today. It worked by identifying the difference between the current state and a goal state.

Enduring Influence on Modern AI

  • Heuristic Search: His idea of using "rules of thumb" to find solutions efficiently is fundamental to everything from GPS navigation to complex game AI.
  • Cognitive Architectures: His work inspired models of the human mind, helping create more human-like AI agents.
  • Symbolic AI: His focus on how machines process symbols laid the conceptual groundwork for Natural Language Processing (NLP).
