Dr. Fei-Fei Li

A Pioneer in Artificial Intelligence

An interactive overview of her life, contributions, and lasting impact on the world of AI.

Early Life & Education

  • Born in Beijing, China, in 1976 and grew up in Chengdu.
  • Immigrated to Parsippany, New Jersey, at age 15, overcoming language and cultural barriers.
  • Earned a Bachelor's in Physics from Princeton University (1999).
  • Completed her Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering at Caltech (2005).

Major Contributions

ImageNet

Dr. Li is the inventor of ImageNet, a groundbreaking dataset containing over 14 million hand-annotated images. This project addressed the critical need for large-scale, high-quality data to train AI models.

ILSVRC

She co-launched the ImageNet Large Scale Visual Recognition Challenge (ILSVRC), an annual competition that became the benchmark for computer vision. The 2012 ILSVRC is widely seen as the event that kick-started the deep learning revolution.

Advocacy for Human-Centered AI

Dr. Li is a passionate advocate for developing AI that is ethical, responsible, and beneficial to all of humanity. She believes technology should augment human capabilities, not replace them.

  • AI4ALL: Co-founder of a nonprofit to increase diversity and inclusion in AI education, especially for underrepresented groups.
  • Stanford HAI: Co-Director of the Stanford Institute for Human-Centered AI, which guides research and policy to improve the human condition.

Influence on Modern AI

The creation of ImageNet directly enabled the deep learning breakthroughs that power many technologies we use today. Her work laid the foundation for modern computer vision.
